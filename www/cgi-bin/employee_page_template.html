System Administrator

Profile

The System Administrator is the backbone of our operational stability, ensuring that our servers, networks, and internal systems run at peak efficiency. While the DevOps team focuses on the deployment pipeline, the SysAdmin ensures the underlying foundation is secure, patched, and resilient. They are the first line of defense against downtime, proactively monitoring system health to solve problems before they impact the team.

Technical Stack & Expertise

This role requires a deep technical grasp of hardware, operating systems, and network protocols. Their expertise includes:
-Server Administration: Managing Linux and Windows environments, including configuration, patching, and user access control.
-Network & Security: Maintaining firewalls, VPNs, and internal networks to protect the company’s digital perimeter.
-Backup & Recovery: Implementing robust disaster recovery strategies to ensure data integrity and business continuity.
-Monitoring & Support: Utilizing enterprise monitoring tools to track performance metrics and provide high-level hardware/software support.

Operational Excellence

A great System Administrator balances technical skill with meticulous organization. They are responsible for managing internal IT assets, automating repetitive maintenance tasks with scripting, and ensuring compliance with industry security standards. By documenting infrastructure changes and managing vendor relationships, they provide a reliable environment that allows the rest of the engineering team to focus entirely on building and shipping product.
